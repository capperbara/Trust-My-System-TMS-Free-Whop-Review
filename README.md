## Sports Betting Legal Considerations

  

Before joining TMS or any betting service, it's crucial to understand the legal landscape of sports betting.

  

### Current Legal Status in the United States

  

Sports betting legality has evolved significantly since 2018.

  

Previously, the Professional and Amateur Sports Protection Act of 1992 (PASPA) made sports betting illegal in most states.

Only Delaware, Montana, Nevada, and Oregon were grandfathered in with existing sports betting frameworks.

  

However, the Supreme Court struck down PASPA in Murphy v. National Collegiate Athletic Association in 2018, allowing individual states to legalize sports betting.

  

### Current State of Legalization

  

As of January 2024, **37 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** have operational legal sports betting.

North Carolina has also legalized sports betting, with operations beginning later in 2024.

  

**30 states, Washington D.C., and Puerto Rico** offer online sports betting.

The remaining 8 states with legal sports betting require physical sportsbook locations.

  

Since the Supreme Court's decision, over $20 billion has been wagered on sports in the United States as of May 2020.

  

### State-by-State Variations

  

Legal sports betting states include major markets like:

-   **New York**: Launched online betting in January 2022
-   **California**: Still considering legalization measures
-   **Texas**: Currently illegal, but legislation under consideration
-   **Florida**: Legal but facing court challenges

  

Each state has different:

-   **Tax rates** on winnings
-   **Approved operators** and betting platforms
-   **Betting restrictions** (college sports, prop bets, etc.)
-   **Age requirements** (18 vs 21)

  

### Why This Matters for TMS Members

  

TMS provides betting advice and analysis, but members must place bets through legal channels in their jurisdiction.

  

The service doesn't place bets for you - they provide information and recommendations.

  

You're responsible for ensuring your betting activity complies with local laws.

  

### Key Compliance Points:

  

**Verify Local Legality**

-   Check if sports betting is legal in your state
-   Confirm online betting availability if you plan to bet online
-   Understand any restrictions on bet types or sports

  

**Use Licensed Operators**

-   Only bet through state-licensed sportsbooks
-   Avoid offshore or unlicensed betting sites
-   Verify operator credentials before depositing money

  

**Tax Obligations**

-   Sports betting winnings are taxable income

**Age Requirements**
